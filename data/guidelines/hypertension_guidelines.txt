HYPERTENSION MEDICATION MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES
=============================================

Source: Based on AHA/ACC Hypertension Guidelines

BLOOD PRESSURE TARGETS
----------------------
- General population: < 130/80 mmHg

- Diabetes: < 130/80 mmHg
- CKD: < 130/80 mmHg

ACE INHIBITORS (Lisinopril, Enalapril, Ramipril)
------------------------------------------------
- Take at same time daily (morning or bedtime)
- Dry cough is common side effect (switch to ARB if persistent)
- Monitor potassium levels
- Avoid in pregnancy
- May cause elevated creatinine initially (acceptable if < 30% rise)

ARBs (Losartan, Valsartan, Olmesartan)
--------------------------------------
- Alternative to ACE inhibitors (no cough)
- Take consistently at same time
- Monitor potassium
- Avoid in pregnancy

CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKERS (Amlodipine, Nifedipine)
-------------------------------------------------
- Ankle swelling is common
- Amlodipine: Take once daily
- Extended-release: Do not crush or chew
- Avoid grapefruit juice

THIAZIDE DIURETICS (Hydrochlorothiazide, Chlorthalidone)
--------------------------------------------------------
- Take in the morning (to avoid nighttime urination)
- May deplete potassium - eat potassium-rich foods
- Stay hydrated
- Sun sensitivity - use sunscreen

BETA BLOCKERS (Metoprolol, Atenolol, Carvedilol)
------------------------------------------------
- Do not stop abruptly (can cause rebound hypertension)
- Take with food for better absorption
- May mask hypoglycemia symptoms in diabetics
- Can cause fatigue, cold extremities

ADHERENCE TIPS FOR HYPERTENSION
-------------------------------
1. Hypertension is "silent" - take meds even when feeling fine
2. Home BP monitoring reinforces importance
3. Morning dosing often best for once-daily meds
4. Pill organizers help with complex regimens
5. Address side effects promptly (don't just stop)
6. Lifestyle modifications complement medications
7. Regular follow-up maintains motivation
