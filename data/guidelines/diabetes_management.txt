DIABETES MEDICATION MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES
=========================================

Source: Based on American Diabetes Association (ADA) Standards of Care 2024

METFORMIN (First-line therapy)
------------------------------
- Take with meals to reduce GI side effects
- Start low, titrate slowly (500mg once daily → 500mg twice daily → target dose)
- Target dose: 1500-2000mg/day in divided doses
- Monitor kidney function (eGFR) annually
- Hold before contrast procedures if eGFR < 30

SULFONYLUREAS (Glipizide, Glyburide, Glimepiride)
-------------------------------------------------
- Take 30 minutes before meals
- Risk of hypoglycemia - educate patient on symptoms
- Avoid in elderly or those with irregular meal patterns
- Monitor blood glucose regularly

SGLT2 INHIBITORS (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin)
-----------------------------------------------
- Take in the morning (causes increased urination)
- Stay well hydrated
- Monitor for genital infections
- Cardiovascular and renal benefits

GLP-1 RECEPTOR AGONISTS (Semaglutide, Liraglutide)
--------------------------------------------------
- Inject subcutaneously, usually once weekly or daily
- Start at low dose, titrate up
- Common: nausea (usually improves)
- Weight loss benefit

INSULIN
-------
- Basal insulin: Take at same time daily
- Rapid-acting: Take with meals
- Proper storage (refrigerate unopened, room temp when in use)
- Rotate injection sites
- Monitor blood glucose

ADHERENCE TIPS FOR DIABETES
---------------------------
1. Link medication to daily routines (meals, bedtime)
2. Use pill organizers for oral medications
3. Set phone reminders
4. Keep medications visible (not in cabinets)
5. Understand WHY each medication is important
6. Regular A1C monitoring shows medication effectiveness
7. Address cost concerns - generics available for most
