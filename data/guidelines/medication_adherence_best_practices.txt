MEDICATION ADHERENCE BEST PRACTICES
====================================

UNDERSTANDING NON-ADHERENCE
---------------------------
Types:
1. Intentional - Patient decides not to take medication
2. Unintentional - Forgetting, confusion, inability

Common Barriers:
- Forgetfulness (most common)
- Cost/insurance issues
- Side effects
- Complex regimens
- Lack of understanding
- Health literacy
- Depression/cognitive issues
- Cultural beliefs

EVIDENCE-BASED INTERVENTIONS
----------------------------

1. SIMPLIFICATION
   - Once-daily dosing when possible
   - Combination pills
   - Synchronize refills
   - Align with daily routines

2. REMINDERS
   - Phone alarms/apps
   - Pill organizers (weekly)
   - Calendar systems
   - Smart pill bottles
   - Caregiver reminders

3. EDUCATION
   - Explain the "why" for each medication
   - Use teach-back method
   - Written instructions
   - Visual aids
   - Medication lists

4. MONITORING
   - Regular check-ins
   - Pill counts
   - Refill monitoring
   - Self-monitoring logs
   - Biomarker tracking (A1C, BP)

5. SUPPORT SYSTEMS
   - Family involvement
   - Peer support
   - Care coordinators
   - Pharmacist consultations

6. ADDRESSING COST
   - Generic alternatives
   - Patient assistance programs
   - 90-day supplies
   - Therapeutic alternatives
   - Manufacturer coupons

MOTIVATIONAL INTERVIEWING TECHNIQUES
------------------------------------
- Express empathy
- Develop discrepancy
- Roll with resistance
- Support self-efficacy
- Ask open-ended questions
- Affirm patient efforts
- Reflect back concerns
- Summarize discussions

RED FLAGS FOR NON-ADHERENCE
---------------------------
- Declining refills
- Missed appointments
- Uncontrolled disease markers
- "Pharmacy hopping"
- Medication confusion
- Side effect complaints
- Cost concerns mentioned

SPECIAL POPULATIONS
-------------------
Elderly:
- Simplified regimens
- Large print labels
- Easy-open containers
- Caregiver involvement
- Cognitive assessment

Low Health Literacy:
- Plain language
- Visual aids
- Teach-back
- Limit information per visit

Mental Health:
- Screen for depression
- Address motivation
- Routine integration
- Support systems
